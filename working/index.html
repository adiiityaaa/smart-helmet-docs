Working Principle

Step-by-step operation of the Smart Helmet for Rider Safety.

System Overview

The Smart Helmet for Rider Safety consists of two independent modules powered by Arduino UNO R3 boards. The helmet-side module continuously monitors the rider using multiple sensors, while the bike-side module controls the motorcycle ignition through a relay. Both modules communicate wirelessly using HC-05 Bluetooth modules configured in a Master-Slave setup.

Step 1 - Bluetooth Connection & Helmet Detection

When the motorcycle ignition is switched on, the bike-side Arduino powers up and waits for a Bluetooth connection from the helmet module. The helmet module is powered independently using a rechargeable battery pack.

Until a successful Bluetooth connection is established, the relay keeps the ignition disabled. This ensures that the motorcycle cannot be started unless the rider is wearing the helmet.

Step 2 - Alcohol Detection

After both modules are connected, the MQ3 alcohol sensor continuously monitors the rider's breath for the presence of alcohol. If the detected value exceeds the predefined threshold, the helmet module immediately informs the bike module, which keeps the ignition relay in the OFF state, preventing the motorcycle from starting.

Step 3 - Drowsiness Detection

During operation, the eyelid sensor continuously monitors the rider's eye movement. If the rider's eyes remain closed beyond the configured threshold, the system identifies this as a possible case of drowsiness. The bike module then disables the ignition by switching the relay OFF.

Step 4 - Crash Detection

The MPU6050 continuously measures acceleration and gyroscopic data while the motorcycle is in motion. If a sudden impact or abnormal change in orientation exceeds the configured threshold, the event is classified as a possible crash.

Step 5 - Emergency Alert

Once a crash has been detected, buzzers on both the helmet module and the bike module are activated to alert nearby people. This audible alert helps draw immediate attention to the rider and may assist in obtaining faster emergency response.

Status Indication

Two LED indicators and a buzzer are used to provide visual and audible feedback regarding the current operating state of the system. These indicators assist the rider during startup and normal operation.

Indicator System Status
🔴 Red LED + 🟢 Green LED ON Helmet and bike modules are powered on but Bluetooth pairing has not yet been established.
🔴 Red LED ON The ignition relay remains OFF due to an unsafe condition such as unsuccessful Bluetooth pairing, alcohol detection, or drowsiness detection.
🟢 Green LED ON The helmet and bike modules are successfully paired and the ignition relay is enabled, allowing the motorcycle to start.
Single Buzzer Beep Indicates successful system power-on.
Continuous Buzzer A possible crash has been detected. Both helmet and bike module buzzers are activated to alert nearby people.
